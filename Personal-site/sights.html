Tourism in Angola is directly associated with the country's natural beauty. There are several tourist attractions in Angola that can be visited and explored, which are: Kissama National Park, Kalandula Waterfalls, Ruacaná Falls, Mussulo, Miradouro da Lua, Iona National Park, Zambezi River, Namibe Desert, Tundavala, Serra da Leba etc.

Serra da Leba dangerous zigzag road is tourist attraction in Angola. Steep and winding, an Angolan road has become one of the country's postcards and attracts adventurous tourists in search of a beautiful view of the surrounding landscape. Kalandula waterfalls Formerly known as (Duque de Bragança), they are located about 80 km from the city of Malange, capital of the province with the same name, and 420 km from Luanda, the capital of the country. Located on the Lucala River, the most important tributary of the Kwanza River, the longest river in Angola with a thousand kilometers in length.

Namib Desert is the oldest desert in the world, and occupies an area of about 50 000 km², it is larger than Switzerland. A place where the desert and the sea meet, creating landscapes of rare beauty. The Miradouro da Lua is a set of cliffs 40 km south of Luanda, in the municipality of Samba, in Angola. Over time, erosion caused by wind and rain created the lunar-like landscape we see today.
