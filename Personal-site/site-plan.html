Home page

Angola is a country on the west coast of Africa, whose main territory is limited to the north and northeast by the Democratic Republic of Congo, on the east by Zambia, on the south by Namibia and on the west by the Atlantic Ocean. Includes also the exclave of Cabinda, through which it borders the Republic of Congo, to the north.
Angola is a country that is divided into 18 provinces, and its official currency is kwanza.
Angola has numerous natural resources, such as oil, natural gas, copper, phosphate, diamond, zinc, aluminum, gold, iron, uranium, among others, and a rich fauna and flora.
The Official Language of Angola is Portuguese but there are even more other national languages (dialects) which are:Kikongo, Kimbundu, Tchokwe, Umbundo, Mbunda, Kwanyama, Nhaneca, Fiote, Nganguela, umbundu.
Angola's climate compared to other countries is totally different. From October to April: rains and from May to August: drier and with lower temperatures. The interior zone can be divided into 3 areas: North, with high rainfall and high temperatures, Central Plateau, with a dry season and average temperatures of the order of 19°C, South with quite accentuated thermal amplitudes due to the proximity of the Kalahari desert and the influence of tropical air masses.

Culture of Angola

Angola is home to diverse cultures, with different languages, customs and origins. In handicrafts, the variety of materials used stands out. Through wooden figurines, musical instruments, masks for ritual dances, objects of common use, richly ornamented, oil and sand paintings, the Angolan artistic quality is proven, patent in museums, art galleries and fairs. Associated with traditional festivals promoted by local ethnic groups is also a great cultural value and food.
As a product and fact of culture, music constantly intervenes in people's lives. The styles of Angolan music are: Semba, Kizomba, Zouk, Kuduro, Adoço, Kazukuta, Kilapanda and Rebita.
The most popular dish in Angola is funge, a cooked dough made from corn or cassava flour. It can accompany meat or fish. The most commonly used ingredients are cereals grown for centuries in the country, including sorghum, millet and corn, as well as black-eyed peas, lentils, yams, dinhungo (squash) and okra. Among the fruits, the highlights are watermelon, tamarind and múcua (fruit of the baobab tree). Dendê oil (palm oil) is important in the preparation of several recipes.
Like around the world, Carnival is one of the most important cultural activities in Angola. Once a year, everyone in Angola observes this carnival manifestation.

Sights

Tourism in Angola is directly associated with the country's natural beauty. There are several tourist attractions in Angola that can be visited and explored, which are: Kissama National Park, Kalandula Waterfalls, Ruacaná Falls, Mussulo, Miradouro da Lua, Iona National Park, Zambezi River, Namibe Desert, Tundavala, Serra da Leba etc.
Serra da Leba dangerous zigzag road is tourist attraction in Angola. Steep and winding, an Angolan road has become one of the country's postcards and attracts adventurous tourists in search of a beautiful view of the surrounding landscape.
Kalandula waterfalls Formerly known as (Duque de Bragança), they are located about 80 km from the city of Malange, capital of the province with the same name, and 420 km from Luanda, the capital of the country. Located on the Lucala River, the most important tributary of the Kwanza River, the longest river in Angola with a thousand kilometers in length.
Namib Desert is the oldest desert in the world, and occupies an area of about 50 000 km², it is larger than Switzerland. A place where the desert and the sea meet, creating landscapes of rare beauty.
The Miradouro da Lua is a set of cliffs 40 km south of Luanda, in the municipality of Samba, in Angola. Over time, erosion caused by wind and rain created the lunar-like landscape we see today.
