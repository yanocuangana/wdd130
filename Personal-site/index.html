Angola

A country on the west coast of Africa, whose main territory is limited to the north and northeast by the Democratic Republic of Congo, on the east by Zambia, on the south by Namibia and on the west by the Atlantic Ocean. Includes also the exclave of Cabinda, through which it borders the Republic of Congo, to the north.
Angola's climate compared to other countries is totally different. From October to April: rains and from May to August: drier and with lower temperatures. The interior zone can be divided into areas: North, with high rainfall and high temperatures, Central Plateau, with a dry season and average temperatures of the order of 19°C, South with quite accentuated thermal amplitudes due to the proximity of the Kalahari desert and the influence of tropical air masses.

Angola is a country that is divided into 18 provinces, and its official currency is kwanza. Angola has numerous natural resources, such as oil, natural gas, copper, phosphate, diamond, zinc, aluminum, gold, iron, uranium, among others, and a rich fauna and flora. The Official Language of Angola is Portuguese but there are even more other national languages (dialects) which are:Kikongo, Kimbundu, Tchokwe, Umbundo, Mbunda, Kwanyama, Nhaneca, Fiote, Nganguela, umbundu.
